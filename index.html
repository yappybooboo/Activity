“If the environment is happy, people will laugh and your grief will go away.”
― Srinivas Mishr

Mother Earth has been talking to us ever since her beginning.
She smiles through sunshine, cries through rainfall and screams through storms.
And now, more than ever, paying attention to the many moods of the earth is crucial,
as we face climate change and detrimental weather events that place people at risk.
Life as we know it depends on the health of the planet, and that’s why Earth Day,
which falls on April 22, is so important.
